Every piece of wood carries its own story, and this slab of black walnut was no different. Sourced from a local mill just outside of town, the live edge was left completely intact — the wild, undulating boundary between bark and heartwood tells you exactly where the tree once met the sky.

## The Build

Joinery is all mortise-and-tenon, pinned with walnut dowels. No pocket screws, no compromises. The legs are solid 4" square walnut stock, tapered on the lathe and given a slight splay for stability.

The finish is Rubio Monocoat, applied in two coats and buffed by hand. It deepens the grain without obscuring it — the wood still feels like wood, not furniture-store lacquer.

## Notes for the Client

This table was built for a family in the Pacific Northwest who wanted a piece they could pass down. It comes with two leaves for expansion and a matching bench — both shown in the gallery.
