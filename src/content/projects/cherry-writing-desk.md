Cherry is one of those woods that rewards patience. When it first comes off the saw it looks almost pink and underwhelming. Then it hits the light, and the oil finish goes on, and you see it begin to darken into that deep amber-red that only cherry gets. Three years from now it will look even better.

## Design

The client wanted something period-appropriate for a room with Arts & Crafts furniture, but not a copy of anything specific. The result is somewhere between Stickley and Shaker — clean, honest, slightly tapered legs with a subtle chamfer on the inside faces.

Two drawers on the right, dovetailed by hand. The pulls are walnut, turned on the lathe and finished with danish oil to contrast slightly against the cherry.

## Finish

Waterlox original, three coats, rubbed out with 0000 steel wool and paste wax. Silky to the touch and reversible — always important in a piece that might need refinishing in fifty years.
