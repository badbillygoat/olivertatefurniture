The brief was simple: a reading chair that felt like sitting in the hollow of a driftwood log. That image stayed with me through the whole build.

## The Form

The back and armrests are steam-bent white oak, bent over a form I built specifically for this chair. Steam bending is unforgiving — you have one chance to get the bend right before the wood either sets or breaks. I ran eight test strips before I was satisfied with the curve and ready to commit to the final pieces.

The legs are straight-grained solid oak, slightly raked to the rear, with a subtle taper that keeps the chair feeling light despite its solidity.

## Joinery

All joints are mortise-and-tenon, drawbored with oak pegs. The seat shell is upholstered over a curved plywood form and drops into the frame — it can be removed and re-covered in the future without dismantling the chair.

## Finish

Osmo Polyx in satin, rubbed back with 0000 steel wool between coats. White oak responds beautifully to hardwax oil — the grain opens up and the ray flecks catch the light differently depending on where you're standing.
